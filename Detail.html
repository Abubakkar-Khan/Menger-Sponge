What is the Menger Sponge?

A Menger sponge is a 3D fractal shape that starts with a simple cube. This cube is divided into 27 smaller cubes (like a Rubik's cube), and the process of removing cubes begins. The center cube of each face and the middle cube of the entire structure are removed, leaving 20 smaller cubes in the next iteration.

This process is repeated recursively, where each smaller cube undergoes the same division and removal of cubes. As each iteration progresses, the sponge becomes more intricate and detailed, with smaller cubes removed in each step, creating a highly porous structure.

Despite its increasing surface complexity, the volume of the Menger sponge approaches zero as the iterations go on. This paradoxical property makes it an interesting object in mathematics, used to demonstrate concepts like fractals, infinite surfaces, and self-similarity in geometry.
